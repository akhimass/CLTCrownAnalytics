# CLTCrownAnalytics

**Sports business analytics for Charlotte’s arena economy** — built around **Charlotte Crown** (women’s professional basketball, **UpShot League**), with market context from **Charlotte FC**, **Charlotte Knights**, and the **Charlotte Checkers**.

## In one minute

Charlotte Crown opens in a city that already has MLS, Triple-A baseball, and AHL hockey. This project answers planning questions in plain business terms: *what moves tickets*, *what same-night games cost you*, *how hard is the building to reach from UNCC*, and *what do promo and price scenarios do to revenue*. Crown is modeled as a **new** franchise in a **mature** market — peer data fills gaps until real gate history exists.

## What the analysis emphasizes

- **Demand drivers** — survey-adjusted weights for promos, price, transit, social, and star power (Year 1 priors; refine after gates open).
- **Revenue scenarios** — baseline vs stronger promo/pricing vs shuttle and marketing layers (illustrative until real attendance is wired in).
- **Competition** — time-aware conflict impact when MLS or MiLB hosts the same day as Crown — tuned for limited overlap with Crown’s price point and audience.
- **Transit** — UNCC → Bojangles vs Blue Line peers; last-mile gap and game-day shuttle framing (CTC hub).

Supporting chart exports for decks live in **`reports/presentation/`** (e.g. conflict table, transit shuttle, driver comparison).
